# Urgent Symptoms and Escalation Guide

If any of the following are reported, treat the situation as potentially urgent and advise immediate notification of the nurse or doctor.

## Potentially Urgent Symptoms

- chest pain
- trouble breathing
- blue lips or face
- severe or rapidly worsening pain
- sudden confusion or inability to stay awake
- loss of consciousness
- heavy bleeding
- vomiting that will not stop
- seizure-like activity
- severe allergic reaction symptoms
  - facial swelling
  - tongue swelling
  - wheezing
  - hives with breathing trouble
- inability to move a limb suddenly
- new one-sided weakness
- very high fever if clinically concerning
- severe wound drainage, spreading redness, or foul-smelling discharge

## High Concern But Not Always Immediate Emergency

- uncontrolled nausea
- inability to urinate when expected postop
- severe constipation with pain
- increasing swelling
- dizziness with near-fainting
- medication side effects causing major discomfort
- pain not controlled by current plan
- sudden severe headache after surgery

## Agent Behavior

If an urgent symptom is present:
- say the concern may be urgent
- recommend alerting the nurse or doctor immediately
- avoid giving definitive medical conclusions
- help summarize the concern in 1-2 sentences

## Suggested Escalation Phrases

For patients:
- "Please alert your nurse right away."
- "These symptoms may need immediate attention from your care team."

For nurses:
- "This should be prioritized for clinical evaluation."
- "The symptom pattern may warrant immediate review."
